The Art of Cable Management for a Clean Battlestation

A clean setup isn't just for looks—it can improve airflow and reduce distractions. Here are our top tips for taming the cable monster.

A powerful gaming rig can quickly become a tangled mess of wires. Power cords, display cables, USB connectors, and audio jacks can create a 'cable spaghetti' situation that's not only ugly but can also restrict airflow and make cleaning a nightmare.

Mastering cable management is the final step in creating a truly professional-looking battlestation.

Start with a Clean Slate

Start by unplugging everything. Yes, everything. This gives you a fresh perspective and allows you to plan your cable routing from scratch. Take photos of your current setup before disconnecting if you're worried about forgetting where things go.

Group cables together based on their destination (e.g., all monitor cables, all peripheral cables). This will help you visualize the most efficient routing paths.

Essential Cable Management Tools

Before you start routing, gather these essential tools:

  • Velcro ties - Reusable and adjustable, perfect for bundling cables
  • Zip ties - Permanent solution for cables that won't change
  • Cable clips - Attach to desk edges to guide individual cables
  • Under-desk cable tray - Hides power strips and excess cable length
  • Braided cable sleeves - Combines multiple cables into one clean tube

Routing Strategy

Use velcro ties or zip ties to bundle them neatly. Route these bundles along the back of your desk or through built-in cable channels. Most modern desks have some form of cable management built-in—use it!

Under-desk cable trays or raceways are fantastic investments for hiding power strips and excess wiring. They keep everything off the floor and easily accessible for future changes.

The Finishing Touches

For the cables on your desk, consider braided sleeves to combine them into a single, clean-looking tube. This is especially effective for your keyboard, mouse, and headset cables.

Cable clips attached to your desk edge can guide individual cables exactly where you want them, preventing them from falling behind your desk when unplugged.

Maintenance and Future-Proofing

Leave some slack in your cable runs for future adjustments. Use velcro ties instead of zip ties wherever possible—they're much easier to adjust when you inevitably want to add or change something.

It takes time and patience, but the result is a setup that looks incredible, is easier to maintain, and lets you focus on what truly matters: the game.

Pro Tips

  • Label your cables at both ends—you'll thank yourself later
  • Keep power and data cables separated to avoid interference
  • Route cables away from heat sources like your PC exhaust
  • Consider wireless peripherals to reduce cable count
  • Plan for RGB lighting cables if you're into that aesthetic
